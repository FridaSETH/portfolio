Nytti - Redesign of Medication Management as a Lifestyle Product

Keywords: strategy, lifestyle product, UX, human-centered design, usability

This project reimagines daily medication management as a lifestyle-oriented experience. Developed in the Strategic Design course at NTNU for entrepreneur Hallgrim Bjørvik, “Nytti” combines a physical product and an app to reduce stigma, improve usability, and support long-term routines.

This project was conducted for Bjørvik at the very beginning of his start-up. Today, his startup has evolved into Dtablet, and you can view the company page here: https://www.dtablet.no/

Fellow students: Caroline Foss Nersnæs, Vilde Hvam og Ellen Sofie Myrmo-Silset

INTRODUCTION

By focusing on usability, personalization, and lifestyle integration, the concept Nytti aims to reduce friction in medication routines, support daily habits, and empower users to maintain independence.

INITIAL BRIEF

Bjørvik’s startup wanted to develop an innovative product line addressing challenges related to taking medication by combining design and technology for safe and effective medication storage.

APPROACH & PROCESS

After receiving the brief, we conducted an internal analysis followed by an external analysis to better understand users, the market, product-related practicalities, and future healthcare trends.

Throughout the process we have made use of several methods. The insight provided derives from a mixture of literature reviews, qualitative and quantitative interviews, email correspondence and client meetings.

The insights were synthesized using design and strategy tools such as personas, problem scenarios, MoSCoW matrixes, competitor analyses, STEEP analyses and mind mapping. This painted a picture of current conditions and future scenarios, which served as a foundation for the strategic direction.

THE STRATEGY

The result of this project is a strategic concept for “Nytti”, a lifestyle-oriented medication management product that evolves over time through three short-, mid- and long-term development phases: a physical box with strong branding, a supporting app, and an advanced all-in-one system. Together, these elements form a roadmap that balances short-term feasibility with long-term innovation, aiming to build user trust, reduce stigma, and promote routine through thoughtful design.

THE VISION

The vision of Nytti is to create a solution that makes medication routines effective, safe, and give users a sense of control. The concept aims to support and motivate people who have recently received the diagnosis, helping them integrate medication into daily life in a way that feels less intimidating or stressful.

TARGET AUDIENCE

The primary focus group consists of recently diagnosed users who are adjusting to taking daily medication.

At this stage, users are often in the process of establishing new routines and adapting to lifestyle changes. Nytti aims to support this transition by offering an easy-to-adopt, low-threshold solution that helps users build sustainable habits around their medication routines.

The broader target audience includes anyone who takes medication frequently and could benefit from improved organization, safer storage, and a more user-friendly experience.

THE BRAND STRATEGY

By focusing on building a strong brand Nytti aims to:
• Develop a visual identity that clearly communicates the company’s values to the target audience
• Build trust and spark interest, ultimately motivating the user to purchase the product
• Create a memorable brand presence that stands out in the market

A set of core values forms the foundation for the development of the visual identity and overall product experience.

Desirability - Create products that people want to own, rather than feel obliged to use.
Empathy - Respect the personal nature of medication management by designing with dignity, understanding, and care.
Simplicity - Ensure every interaction is intuitive, from opening the box to managing or swapping modules.
Authenticity - Communicate through honest and transparent branding and product storytelling rooted in real user needs.
